VR Graffiti Stealth Game - Wrong Side of da Tracks

Description


Players take on the role of a passionate underground graffiti artist whose goal is to leave their artistic marks on the subway station without being caught. The artist sneaks into the empty subway station through a hidden entrance and explores the surrounding environment, using the structure and routes of the subway station, cleverly avoiding surveillance, to find suitable locations for graffiti and paint. After completing the graffiti, the artist needs to catch the first departing train in the morning to escape the subway to win the game.


Inspiration


Bristol, as a graffiti-rich city has become a source of creative inspiration for our project. The graffiti not only gives the city's urban landscape more character but also demonstrates the writers' passion for free expression, opposing the current legislations around the activity. Studying in Bristol got us thinking how compelling it would be to translate the spirit of these writers into an immersive gaming experience that allows players to explore and create in a virtual space.

Responsibilities & Related Skills

1. Designed game mechanics, narrative, and level structure, ensuring an immersive and cohesive experience.
2. Developed VR hand animations, poses and trigger logics based on different interactions, implementing UI, spray can interactions and paint mechanic using VR techniques in Unity (C#).
3. Optimised real-time spray paint rendering and interaction, achieving fluid VR performance with particle effects.
4. Modelled assets in Maya, and procedurally generated environment assets to create parallax effect, enhancing visual engagement while reducing storage requirements.
5. Implemented AI behaviours with Behaviour Trees, enhancing NPC decision-making and game dynamics.
